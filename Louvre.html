The Louvre Museum holds many famous artworks and is the world's largest museum and a historic monument in Paris, France. A central landmark of the city, it is located on the Right Bank of the Seine on 75001 Paris, France.

The museum was originally built as a fortress in the late 12th century but now showcases 35,000 works of art, from Mesopotamian, Egyptian and Greek artworks to masterpieces by artists such as da Vinci, Michelangelo and Rembrandt. It would take nine months to glance at every piece. The museum opened on 10 August 1793 with an exhibition of 537 paintings, the majority of the works being royal and confiscated church property.

One of the most famous artworks within the museum is the 'Mona Lisa'. The 'Mona Lisa' was painted by Leonardo da Vinci.

The Louvre has three entrances: the main entrance at the pyramid, an entrance from the Carrousel du Louvre underground shopping mall, and an entrance at the Porte des Lions. Under the main entrance to the museum is the Carrousel du Louvre, a shopping mall.
